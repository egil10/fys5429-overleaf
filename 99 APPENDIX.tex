The log-Euler update for $S$ is driven by a Brownian increment $Z_1$, correlated with the variance-driving increment $Z_2=\rho Z_1+\sqrt{1-\rho^2}Z_2^\perp$ as in \parencite{andersen2008simple}. The negative leverage correlation $\rho=-0.7$ produces a left-skewed terminal price distribution (right panel) and heavier left tails than the lognormal benchmark above.}

        \caption{Corresponding Heston instantaneous volatility $\sqrt{v_t}$ paths under the same parameter set. The reflection-Euler scheme $v_{t+\Delta t}=|v_t+\kappa(\theta-v_t)\Delta t+\xi\sqrt{v_t\Delta t}\,Z_2|$ from \parencite{andersen2008simple} discretizes the CIR variance dynamics of \parencite{cox1985cir} while keeping $v_t\ge 0$. The white dashed line marks the long-run target $\sqrt{\theta}=0.20$; paths mean-revert toward this level on a timescale $1/\kappa=0.5$ year, with the Feller condition $2\kappa\theta=0.16>\xi^2=0.09$ comfortably satisfied \parencite{feller1951two}.}

\subsection{Black--Scholes PINN}

The Black--Scholes hyperparameter study consists of four sweeps run for $10{,}000$ epochs each on the standard collocation grid (interior, IC, BC). The sweeps varied (i)~depth\,$\times$\,width\,$\times$\,activation (18 combinations over $\{2,3,4\}$ layers, $\{64,128,256\}$ neurons, and \texttt{softplus}/\texttt{tanh}), (ii)~the Adam learning rate, (iii)~the loss-weight triple $(\lambda_{\text{PDE}},\lambda_{\text{IC}},\lambda_{\text{BC}})$, and (iv)~the epoch budget.

\clearpage\subsection{Heston PINN}

The Heston PINN was selected from analogous sweeps over depth/width/activation, learning rate, $\lambda$ triples, and epoch budget on the three-dimensional $(S,v,\tau)$ collocation grid. The best configuration uses three hidden layers of 256 \texttt{tanh} neurons with $(\lambda_{\text{PDE}},\lambda_{\text{IC}},\lambda_{\text{BC}})=(10,10,5)$ and $\eta_0=5\times 10^{-3}$ under the same cosine-annealing schedule \parencite{loshchilov2017sgdr}. The per-component loss curves below show the corresponding 50{,}000-epoch run, with the larger Heston state space producing markedly noisier early-training transients than the Black--Scholes case.

The weighted total falls from $\approx 2.5\times 10^{5}$ at initialization (dominated by the upper-$S$ Dirichlet BC) to $\approx 0.92$ at epoch $50{,}000$, with intermittent excursions around epochs $15{,}000$--$22{,}500$ where a transient instability inflates the PDE and BC components by an order of magnitude before the cosine-annealed learning rate damps them.}

    \end{subfigure}
    \hfill
    \begin{subfigure}[t]{0.49\linewidth}

\clearpage\subsection{Inverse PINN}

The inverse PINN was trained on a synthetic Heston dataset of 200 noisy price observations spread across the $(S,v,\tau)$ domain, with the four Heston parameters reparameterized via softplus/\texttt{tanh} (Listing~\ref{lst:reparam}). Optimization used Adam for 25{,}000 epochs followed by 5{,}000 L-BFGS \parencite{liu1989limited} refinement steps, with $(\lambda_{\text{PDE}},\lambda_{\text{IC}},\lambda_{\text{BC}},\lambda_{\text{data}},\lambda_{\text{Feller}})=(20,10,5,1000,0.1)$, NN learning rate $\eta_0=2\times 10^{-3}$, and a separate Heston-parameter learning rate $\eta_{\text{Heston}}=10^{-3}$. The two figures below trace the loss decomposition and the parameter trajectories; the final calibrated values $(\kappa,\theta,\xi,\rho)=(1.588,0.0266,0.104,0.994)$ should be compared with the data-generating truth $(2.0,0.04,0.3,-0.7)$ from Table~\ref{tab:inverse_pinn_calibration_history}.
